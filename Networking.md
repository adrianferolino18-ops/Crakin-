# My Understanding of Networking & The Internet

### What is Networking?
To me, **Networking** is simply the way things are connected. You can see it in daily life:
* **The Social Link**: You are connected to a friend because of shared hobbies. That friend might have another friend who can help you find a job. That "chain" of people is a network.
* **The Device Link**: Computers work the same way. You can't print a photo without first downloading it from Google using a phone or PC, and you can't print it without being connected to the printer. These devices must "talk" to each other to get the job done.


### What is the Internet?
If a single network is like a small group of friends, the **Internet** is what happens when you bring all those separate groups together.

Imagine two different friend groups (these are **Local Networks**). Each group has its own inside jokes and ways of talking. If you belong to both groups, you act as the bridge to introduce them. 

However, they might not "speak the same language" yet. One group uses slang the other doesn't understand. This is where the **Router** comes in:

* **The Connection**: You are the physical link that brings the two circles together.
* **The Protocol**: When Group A wants to tell a joke to Group B, they tell it to you first.
* **The Translation**: You interpret and "translate" that message so the other group understands it perfectly.


### Summary
The **Internet** is just millions of these small "friend groups" (networks) all connected by Routers. These routers make sure that a message starting in one small circle can find its way to any other circle in the world.
